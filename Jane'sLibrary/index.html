About Me


I am an avid reader and generally read a book a week. I regularly read NPR and Lambda Literary to discover new books to read.


As a native New Yorker, I love to read books about New York history, such as Robin Schulman's Eat The City, which is about the fishers, butchers, farmers, beekeepers, and brewers who built this city. As someone who became a dog person later in life, I loved Benoit Denizen-Lewis's Travels with Casey, which details his cross-country trip with his dog and the other dog lovers he meets along the way. As a former editor and writer who wrote many personality profiles, I also love memoirs, such as Shouting Won't Help, a memoir about hearing loss by Katherine Bouton. I also read a fair amount about music, including David Byrne's How Music Works.


I've worked in the non-profit sector for a number of years. For books, articles, websites and databases pertaining to the non-profit sector, you can't go wrong with The Foundation Center. They have information about all aspects of fundraising and non-profit management, not just foundations.


I am also interested in technology, and it's important to me that everyone be able to use the Internet. I work at Holy Apostles Soup Kitchen and we redesigned our website in 2014. One of things we did was optimize the website for mobile and test the website in older browsers. We haven't received any complaints about our donation or volunteer forms since the redesign.


This website was created for my final project at General Assembly's Front-End Web Development course. In the summer of 2015, I took Digital Marketing at the same school.
